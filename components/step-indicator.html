Step Indicator Component

A step indicator is a visual component used to show a user's progress through a multi-step process, such as a checkout flow or a form wizard. This component helps to orient users and gives them a clear sense of how far they have progressed and how many steps are remaining.

Passed WCAG 2.1 AA

Interactive Example

Standard Step Indicator

  • 1 Completed Step
  • 2 Current Step
  • 3 Next Step

Source Code

<nav aria-label="Progress">
    <ul class="step-indicator">
        <li class="completed">
            <span class="step-indicator-number">1</span>
            <span class="step-indicator-label">Completed Step</span>
        </li>
        <li class="current">
            <span class="step-indicator-number">2</span>
            <span class="step-indicator-label">Current Step</span>
        </li>
        <li>
            <span class="step-indicator-number">3</span>
            <span class="step-indicator-label">Next Step</span>
        </li>
    </ul>
</nav>

Guidance

Step indicators are most effective when they clearly show the progress and status of a multi-step process. They should be placed prominently at the top of the content and be consistent across all steps.

When to use a step indicator component

  • Multi-step forms. To guide users through a complex form, such as a registration or payment process.
  • Checkouts. For e-commerce sites to show customers their progress through the purchasing process.

Usability tips

  • Clear states. Use distinct visual styles to differentiate between completed, current, and future steps.
  • Descriptive labels. Each step should have a clear label that explains its purpose (e.g., "Shipping", "Payment", "Review").

Accessibility Features

  • Nav Landmark. The nav element with aria-label="Progress" provides a clear landmark role for the component.
  • Current State. The `aria-current="step"` attribute on the current list item is used to inform screen readers of the active step in the process.
  • Semantic List. Using a "ul" and "li" structure provides a logical and understandable format for assistive technologies.

Testing

To ensure the step indicator component meets accessibility standards, refer to the Step Indicator Component Testing Checklist.
