Banner Component

A banner is used to display an important message to users on a webpage, often used for announcements or alerts.

Passed WCAG 2.1 AA

Standard Banners

Reminder: Preheat your oven!

Before you start baking, ensure your oven is preheated to the correct temperature. This guarantees a perfect rise and a golden crust.

Warning: Watch your bake time!

Your cookies may burn if you bake them for too long. Check them two minutes before the timer goes off.

Success! Your cake is baked.

Congratulations! Your cake has successfully baked and is now ready for cooling and frosting.

Guidance

When to use the banner component

  • Announcements. Use banners to announce new features or important updates to users.
  • Critical messages. Deliver critical information that needs immediate attention.

Usability tips

  • Clarity is key. Ensure the message is clear and concise to convey the main point quickly.
  • Effective design. Use contrasting colors to ensure the banner stands out on the page without being jarring.

Accessibility guidance

Ensure your banner component is accessible by providing text alternatives and using proper ARIA roles.

  • Utilize role="banner" for defining the component and enhance discoverability for assistive technologies.

Testing

To ensure the banner component meets accessibility standards, refer to the Banner Component Testing Checklist.
