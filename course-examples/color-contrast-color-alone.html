Color Contrast

Color contrast: Is the difference in lightness/darkness between two colors (background and foreground). Text, icons, and important graphics must have sufficient color contrast between colors for users who have low vision or who are colorblind.




Non-Text Contrast



Recap

When testing for color contrast, note that the combination of state branding Minnesota green and white will not be accessible at any point in size or non-text element.

Color Contrast (WCAG 1.4.3):

Recap: Ensures that text has sufficient contrast against its background to be readable by users with visual impairments. The minimum contrast ratio is 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text.

Best Practices for testing: Use a color contrast analyzer to measure the contrast ratio. Ensure you test change states on the non-text elements and text over color gradient backgrounds.

Non-Text Contrast (WCAG 1.4.11):

Recap: Ensures that graphical objects and user interface components have sufficient contrast against their background to be distinguishable.

Best Practices for testing:

    • Use a color contrast analyzer to measure the contrast ratio. The ratio must be at least 3:1 for user interface components like buttons, form fields, and focus indicators.
    • Verify that essential visual information is not lost due to insufficient contrast. Check to ensure the icons with a purpose or meaning have enough contrast. Think search or menu icons.
    • Share that Minnesota's green and white combination will not pass color contrast.
